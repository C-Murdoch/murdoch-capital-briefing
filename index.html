1. Global Macro

  • The US-Iran geopolitical crisis dominated macro sentiment overnight, with the US and Iran agreeing to a conditional two-week ceasefire after Trump threatened strikes on civilian infrastructure if the Strait of Hormuz remained closed. Iran agreed to guarantee safe passage through the Strait for the ceasefire duration.
  • US CPI for March 2026 came in at 2.8% year-on-year (headline) and 3.1% annually on core, with core rising 0.4% month-on-month. Sticky core inflation has pushed market-implied probability of a June Fed rate cut from approximately 55% to 35%.
  • The Federal Reserve held the federal funds rate at 3.50%-3.75% at its March meeting, with the median official projection now calling for just one cut in 2026. The Fed revised its 2026 GDP growth forecast down to 0.9% and lifted its headline PCE inflation projection to 2.7%.

  • The Bank of England also held at 3.75%, reflecting a similarly cautious posture across major developed-market central banks as geopolitical supply shocks complicate the inflation outlook.

  • ASX 200 futures pointed to only a 0.1% gain at the open, though the ceasefire news - breaking after Australian hours - is likely to drive a significantly stronger open on Thursday.

Key Themes for Today

The US-Iran ceasefire is the defining event of the week, triggering a sharp reversal in oil prices and broad equity relief rally - but the two-week window means markets remain on a knife-edge and volatility premiums should stay elevated. Against this backdrop, investors must also contend with the CPI data confirming sticky US core inflation at 3.1%, which materially reduces the probability of a June Fed rate cut and keeps real rates high at a time when geopolitical risk could still re-escalate.
